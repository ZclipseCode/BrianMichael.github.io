Nosediver

Steve is nosediving from his office, but you can help him! Guide Steve around to avoid crates and birds while also collecting pizzas and power-ups. Any pizzas that don't land on the roofs can be used to purchase new characters.

Initially created for a game jam, Nosediver was my first solo project. I wanted to create something achieveable given my skill-level at the time, so I went with an arcade-style score game. To make things more exciting, I added a customization shop as well as power-ups. Nosediver was made in Unity.

Cowboy's Chorus

You're an outlaw and as such have just robbed a bank. Numerous cowboys are in your pursuit in order to collect your bounty. After you fend them off, only the sheriff stands in your way...

Cowboy's Chorus was a game developed for the University of Wisconsin-Whitewater Media Arts and Game Development Expo 2023, my C# final, and a game jam. The idea behind this was to create a game that could be enjoyed regardless of the user's level of visibility. The game does not require any visuals, however they are there in case someone desires aid. Cowboy's Chorus was made in Unity.

Motivational Keyboard

While this app is running in the background, you begin to type and suddenly, you hear a beat. As you continue typing, the beat gains more instruments. As you continue, the music grows and grows. Confidence shines in you as you complete your paper with a full concert formed from your fingertips.

With the Motivational Keyboard, I created an app as opposed to a video game. As the title suggests, the main purpose of this app was to motivate users to type something, whether that be a late paper or an important email. The Motivational Keyboard is a great tool for focus. This app was made in Unity.

Primis

"My professors say that if you just do what you have to do to get by, then you're not going to get very far... You need to put yourself out there."

I was previously employed at Roberta's Art Gallery in the University of Wisconsin-Whitewater campus. For each exhibit, we were responsible for installing the art pieces. This included many measurements that would oftentimes result in confusion. As a result of this, I created a program that did all the calculations for us, making work more efficient. Primis was created in Unity.
